**Why this matters:** Majors extends the verification thesis past code review into production observability. Honeycomb's MCP integration — agents querying their own telemetry — points to an emerging pattern: observability for agents.

### Dave Farley — "Verification Is the New Bottleneck"
**Recent | [Aviator Podcast](https://www.aviator.co/podcast/engineering-discipline-dave-farley) · [CD Training](https://courses.cd.training/)**

On Yegge's 12K LOC/day: **"I can't read that much code carefully enough to feel trust. **Why this matters:** Farley bridges Kent Beck's TDD-as-superpower to production reality. His "executable specifications" concept is the practical answer to Osmani's Comprehension Debt — you don't need to read all the code if specifications are complete and verified.

### Jaana Dogan — "This Isn't Funny"

### Mitchell Hashimoto — The Pragmatic Architect

### ThePrimeagen — The Principled Minimalist
**2026 | [ByteIota](https://byteiota.com/theprimeagens-99-hits-542-stars-day-ai-for-skilled-devs/) · [The Standup Podcast](https://creators.spotify.com/pod/profile/thestanduppod/episodes/Our-Spiciest-2026-Predictions-e3d0mck)**

The market is splitting: **maximalists** (AI does everything) vs **minimalists** (AI constrained to specific tasks). ThePrimeagen leads the minimalist camp, and the 96% distrust figure + 19% slowdown data gives his position credibility.

**Why this matters:** Primeagen represents the senior engineer who adopts AI on their own terms. His "99" plugin philosophy embodies discipline over demo — a principled approach to AI-augmented development for experienced engineers.

### Bryan Cantrill — "Yes, Even in an Era of Vibe Coding"
**Recent | [Oxide Blog](https://oxide.computer/blog/systems-software-in-the-large) · [Oxide and Friends Podcast](https://oxide-and-friends.transistor.fm/)**

Oxide CTO, DTrace creator. On systems software: **"Software is hard (yes, even in an era of vibe coding), and systems software is especially so. The expectation for foundational software is rightly perfection — a merely functional system can be deceptively distant from the robustness required."**

Predicted "vibe coding" will be out of the lexicon within a year — or used strictly pejoratively, becoming "a named condition." Hosted Simon Willison on Oxide and Friends for LLM predictions (Willison predicted: "within a year it will become undeniable that LLMs write good code").

**Why this matters:** Cantrill is a contrarian anchor for infrastructure/systems work. His position validates an important nuance: AI transforms application-level development but systems software remains a different beast.

### Kelsey Hightower — "Do Not Lose Your Mind"
**Recent | [TechTarget](https://www.techtarget.com/searchitoperations/video/Kelsey-Hightowers-advice-for-platform-engineers-as-AI-looms) · [TFiR](https://tfir.io/kelsey-hightower-on-life-after-google-open-source-business-models-and-the-future-of-ai/)**

Retired Google Distinguished Engineer. AI is a **"surface-level" technology** — it operates on servers, networks, GPUs, programming languages, protocols. The fundamentals don't change.

On AI agent overreach: **"If a user loses their password, allow them to reset it if they're still employed by looking in the HR database. You do not need AI or an AI agent to reset passwords. Oh my God, people, please."** Simple programs for simple tasks — cheaply and reliably.

Warning: **"Do not lose your mind because you've forgotten the ways of the Jedi."**

**Why this matters:** Hightower is the voice of engineering sanity. His "surface-level technology" framing complements the electricity analogy — electricity reorganized factories but didn't change physics. An important reminder: not everything needs AI.

### Chelsea Troy — "60% Drek Rate"
**Ongoing | [chelseatroy.com](https://chelseatroy.com/2024/05/26/how-does-ai-impact-my-job-as-a-programmer/)**

ML engineer, University of Chicago educator. Measured LLM output at a **60% "drek rate"** — modulated by user skill. Her grad students caught 70% of the drek, letting 30% slip through. Net: ~1 in 5 chance of garbage with semi-knowledgeable human review.

Key insight: what makes AI useful isn't prompting skill but **actual programming skill and existing subject matter expertise**. The core value humans add: "figure out why the code we already have isn't doing the thing" — reading, analyzing, understanding, and repairing code.

**Why this matters:** Troy's 60% drek rate is the empirical counter to "AI writes 90% of code" claims. Her thesis: programming skill > prompting skill — experienced engineers get better results from AI.
